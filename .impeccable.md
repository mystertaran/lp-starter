## Product

**The Fabos** is a multi-channel e-commerce operations platform. The brand is written with a lowercase `the` and an uppercase `FABOS` wordmark in the logo; in prose use **The Fabos**. It consolidates sales channels, warehouse, orders, shipping, returns/claims, and accounting into one cockpit — turning operational chaos into order.

## Design Context

### Users

- **Primary**: growth-stage multi-channel e-commerce merchants (multiple sales channels, one or more warehouses, enough order volume that spreadsheets and channel-specific tools have stopped scaling).
- **Context of use**: landing page visited during active vendor evaluation. Visitor arrived via ad, referral, or search; they are likely comparing 2–3 platforms and are time-poor.
- **Decision maker profile**: founder/ops lead / head of e-commerce; treats tooling as a cost-vs-risk decision, not an aesthetic one. Skeptical of marketing fluff.

### Job to be Done

**One conversion: request sales contact.** Everything on the page funnels there. No newsletter, no gated download, no demo self-scheduler as the hero CTA. The form is the goal; the copy earns the right to fill it in.

### Brand Personality

Three words The Fabos IS:
- **Operacyjny** — talks about inventory, returns, and VAT like someone who has actually shipped orders. Plain nouns, not slogans.
- **Rzeczowy** — short sentences, concrete numbers, no superlatives.
- **Sprawny** — the product makes work fast, and the page should feel fast too. No heavy scroll-jacking.

Emotional target for hero section:
- **Moc** — a sense that this tool can actually handle their scale.
- **Prostota** — visually quiet, easy to parse on the first pass.
- **Przewaga** — reader should leave believing they will *outrun* the competition by using it.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Kind**: editorial-operational. Think *Financial Times front page* crossed with a well-designed ERP cockpit. Bold typographic hierarchy, generous whitespace, monochrome foundation, one decisive accent.
- **Theme**: light. Warm-tinted neutrals (not pure white, not fintech dark). Trust + commerce lean, same family as Shopify and Revolut but with more typographic weight.
- **Typography**: a heavy condensed display face paired with a refined neutral grotesk for body. Logo already sets the direction — honor it.
  - Display: **Archivo Black** (Google Fonts) — closest free match to the logo's condensed-heavy energy.
  - Body: **Archivo** (same family, variable) — refined, neutral, strong at body sizes.
  - **Banned**: Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Space Grotesk, IBM Plex anything, Geist (all AI-default reflexes). See Impeccable's reflex_fonts_to_reject for the full list.
- **Color**:
  - Accent: `#FF4C00` (signal orange). Used sparingly — CTAs, one-word emphasis, progress markers. Not on large surfaces.
  - Neutrals: tint toward warm gray (slight orange/yellow lean in the neutrals, chroma ~0.005–0.01) for subconscious cohesion with the accent.
  - All colors expressed in **OKLCH**. No hard `#000` / `#fff` — always tint.
- **Motion**: purposeful, not constant. One well-orchestrated hero entrance with staggered reveals. Ease-out-expo/quint. No bounce, no elastic, no parallax. Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### Anti-References

These are the tropes the page must **NOT** adopt:
- A certain Polish multi-channel e-commerce integrator whose site is visually loud, color-saturated, feature-listy and stock-photo-heavy. We are its opposite: quiet, confident, typographic.
- Fintech-glow (dark background + neon gradients + glass cards).
- AI-slop aesthetics: purple-to-blue gradients, cards-in-cards, icon-above-every-heading templating, sparklines as decoration, gradient text (banned entirely), side-stripe borders on callouts (banned entirely).
- Corporate stock photography of smiling office workers. If people are shown, they come from real product/warehouse context.

### Copy Language

**Polish**. No English fallback in v1 — if an English version becomes needed later, it's a separate page, not a language switcher that dilutes the primary copy.

### Accessibility

**WCAG 2.2 AA** across the board. Non-negotiables:
- Color contrast AA on every text/background pair (including orange accent — verify against both light and tinted neutral backgrounds).
- Visible focus ring on every interactive element.
- Form labels always visible, never placeholder-only.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored — disable the staggered hero entrance and any scroll-linked motion.
- Every interactive control reachable by keyboard; tab order matches visual order.

### Analytics

**Google Analytics 4**, measurement-ID in `NEXT_PUBLIC_GA_MEASUREMENT_ID`. Events we care about on v1:
- `page_view` (automatic)
- `hero_cta_click` — primary CTA clicked
- `contact_form_start` — first input focused
- `contact_form_submit` — server action succeeded
- `scroll_depth` — 25/50/75/100%

Use `@next/third-parties/google` for the GA loader, not a raw gtag snippet. Events emitted through a typed `trackEvent(name, params)` helper in `src/lib/analytics.ts`.

## Design Principles

1. **Typography carries the design** — before adding a graphic, see if a heavier weight and a blank line will do the job. The page should work in black and white.
2. **One decision per screen** — every section has exactly one next action for the reader. If two CTAs compete, one is wrong.
3. **Receipts over adjectives** — numbers, integrations, channels. No "innowacyjny", no "rewolucyjny", no "lider".
4. **Orange is rare and earned** — used to mark conversion moments and nothing else. If it appears everywhere, it means nothing.
5. **Fast over fancy** — LCP ≤ 2.0s on 3G, INP ≤ 200ms. Any animation that pushes those numbers gets cut.
